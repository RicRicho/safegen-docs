# 6. Regulatory Allignment

SafeGen is designed to meet age-assurance laws **without identity exposure, document handling, telco data sharing, biometrics, or surveillance**.\
It aligns with every requirement regulators impose while removing the legal, technical, and privacy conflicts that block other solutions.

SafeGen provides **enforcement without identification**, **auditability without personal data**, and **national-scale compliance without centralised identity systems**.

***

## **6.1 Compliance with Australian Age-Assurance Requirements**

Australia’s emerging age-assurance frameworks require:

* Preventing under-16 access to certain services
* Ensuring platforms take “reasonable, proportionate, and technically effective steps”
* Avoiding excessive data collection
* Preserving privacy rights
* Providing audit trails to regulators

SafeGen satisfies all five requirements:

**1. Enforceable minor classification**\
Platforms receive a cryptographically signed _“minor = YES/NO”_ signal — sufficient to enforce the law.

**2. No excessive data**\
No DOBs, IDs, photos, biometrics, or subscriber data are ever captured or shared.

**3. Privacy-preserving by architecture**\
Data minimisation is structural, not policy-based:\
only salted, irreversible hashes exist inside SafeGen.

**4. Supports platform obligations**\
Platforms can block, limit, or redirect minors based on a verifiable signal.

**5. Regulator-ready auditability**\
Audit logs confirm the presence of valid enforcement signals without revealing any identity.

SafeGen aligns naturally with:

* The Online Safety Act
* Age-assurance codes from eSafety
* Social media minimum-age proposals
* ISP-based blocking mandates
* Future child-safety standards requiring privacy-preserving conduct

***

## **6.2 How SafeGen Avoids Prohibited Data Sharing**

The most sensitive problem in age assurance is that **telcos cannot legally disclose subscriber data**, even in de-identified form.

SafeGen solves this entirely.

**Telcos never export:**

* Phone numbers
* Names
* Dates of birth
* SIM metadata
* Billing records
* Subscriber details

Instead, telcos export only:

* **Salted, irreversible hashes**
* **Signed one-bit statements (“minor = true”)**

This means:

* No customer data crosses institutional boundaries
* Next to no re-identification risk
* No breach liability
* No metadata law conflicts
* No surveillance vector for linking identity with platform behaviour

SafeGen is designed to be compliant because it **physically prevents** identity exposure — not merely promises to avoid it.

***

## **6.3 How SafeGen Enables Enforcement Without ID**

SafeGen allows platforms, ISPs, app stores, and adult sites to enforce age restrictions **without ever identifying a user**.

Mechanism:

1. Platform hashes the phone number the user supplies.
2. Platform sends the hash to SafeGen.
3. SafeGen returns only _minor = YES/NO_.
4. Platform enforces based on the one-bit signal.

At no point is identity:

* requested
* stored
* transmitted
* inferred
* associated with platform activity

This directly satisfies government requirements that age assurance must:

* minimise personal data
* avoid identity collection
* reduce surveillance risk
* avoid centralised identity stores

SafeGen proves that **age can be enforced without identity**.

* **Verification of telco signatures**, without access to subscriber data
* **Independent verification** that SafeGen stores no PII
* **Process audits** demonstrating hashing, salting, and auto-delisting
* **Compliance reports** across platforms, ISPs, app stores, and adult sites

Regulators **do not** see:

* phone numbers
* identities
* dates of birth
* browsing activity
* behavioural data
* any re-identifiable record

This enables:

* national-level compliance monitoring
* enforcement of statutory obligations
* confidence that platforms cannot ignore minor flags
* confidence that SafeGen cannot harvest or misuse identity data

SafeGen gives regulators full visibility of **system integrity** without ever revealing **who any user is**.
