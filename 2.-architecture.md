# 2. Architecture



***

SafeGen’s architecture provides a **single, consistent, privacy-clean age-assurance mechanism** for platforms, telcos, ISPs, and app stores.\
Every component is designed around one principle:

**Enforcement without identity.**\
**Compliance without surveillance.**\
**Zero PII crossing any institutional boundary.**

***

## **2.1 Clean Knowledge Event**

The Clean Knowledge Event (CKE) is the heart of SafeGen.

A CKE is a **one-bit, cryptographically signed fact**:

> **“This hashed number belongs to a minor.”**

Nothing else is shared.\
No identity.\
No **subscriber or identity metadata** (names, numbers, DOBs) in the event itself.\
No telco records.\
No dates of birth.

A CKE solves the hardest legal and technical problem in age assurance:

* Platforms cannot claim “we didn’t know.”
* Telcos do not disclose customer data.
* Regulators can audit the signing without seeing any personal data.

The CKE becomes the **universal enforcement trigger** across:

* Social media
* Adult sites
* App stores
* ISPs
* Telco-ISPs
* Any online service requiring a “minor or adult” determination

***

## **2.2 Notification Layer**

The Notification Layer converts incoming queries into a CKE response.

Flow:

1. A platform or ISP submits a **hashed phone number**.
2. SafeGen matches it against the hashed safelist.
3. If matched, SafeGen returns a signed **“minor = YES”**.
4. If not matched, SafeGen returns **“minor = NO”**.

The Notification Layer:

* Never sees raw phone numbers
* Never stores identity
* Never holds personal records
* Only processes salted hashes
* Returns a **true/false** enforcement signal

This provides:

* Rapid decisioning
* Zero knowledge beyond the yes/no result
* Perfect auditability of the signal itself

***

## **2.3 Hashing & Data Minimisation Model**

SafeGen stores only irreversible, salted, one-way hashes. Raw identifiers are **processed ephemerally in memory and never stored or logged**.

Data minimisation rules:

* **Child’s mobile number → salted hash**
* **Month/year of birth → salted hash**
* **Parent linkage → ephemeral, not stored after verification**
* **Consent logs → non-identifying references**
* **Telco attributes → signed, hashed, anonymised statements**

SafeGen never stores:

* Names
* Phone numbers
* Dates of birth
* Addresses
* Device identifiers
* SIM identifiers
* Billing data
* Biometrics
* Photos
* IDs

Hashes are unique per telco and per integration, preventing correlation and preventing cross-system tracking.

**The hashing model is designed to:**\
• Strongly resist re-identification, assuming salts/keys remain secret\
• Prevent cross-database correlation via per-telco/per-integration salts\
• Minimise breach impact by avoiding storage of raw identifiers or DOBs”

If SafeGen were compromised, attackers would gain **nothing actionable**.

***

## **2.4 Telco Integration Model**

SafeGen can integrate with telcos in two modes:

#### **Mode A: Parent-Driven (Standalone)**

Parents enrol children directly.\
No telco involvement required.

#### **Mode B: Telco-Assisted**

Telcos:

1. Contact their own customers for consent.
2. Generate **hashed, salted mobile numbers** for minors.
3. Produce a **signed, non-identifying age attribute**.
4. Send the **hashed result only** to SafeGen.

Key properties:

* Telcos disclose **no directly identifying customer data**.
* No age, no identity, no subscriber records leave the telco.
* SafeGen receives only a **scrambled, anonymous entry**.

Benefits:

* National-scale coverage
* Population-level accuracy
* Minimal practical privacy risk
* Full compliance with telco privacy and metadata laws

***

## **2.5 Platform Integration Model**

Platforms integrate by adding a **single hash check** during:

* Login
* Registration
* Account recovery
* Phone-number updates
* App installation (for app stores)

Flow:

1. Platform hashes the user-supplied phone number.
2. Sends the hash to SafeGen.
3. Receives **minor: yes/no**.

Platforms never receive:

* Birthdates
* IDs
* Customer data
* Parental information
* Telco data

The platform’s responsibility is minimal:

* Enforce under-16 restrictions when SafeGen says “YES”.
* Log compliance actions for audit.

This replaces complex KYC or biometric flows with a **zero-friction, zero-identity** check.

***

## **2.6 Privacy & Zero-Identity Guarantees**

SafeGen’s architecture is built so that **nobody** — not telcos, not platforms, not SafeGen itself — can ever reconstruct any user’s identity.

Guarantees:

* **No IDs ever uploaded**
* **No face scans**
* **No biometrics**
* **No document checks**
* **No cross-institution data sharing**
* **No raw subscriber data**
* No **raw identifiers or directly identifying personal data** at rest; only salted hashes.
* **No ability to correlate individuals**

SafeGen itself has **structural blindness**:

* It cannot know who a child is.
* It cannot know which telco they belong to.
* It does not need to tie queries to end users or telco identities. Platform identifiers are used only for access control and rate-limiting, not for user tracking.
* It cannot map events to identities.

This design ensures SafeGen cannot become (and cannot be abused as) a centralised identity system.

***

## **2.7 Auto-Delisting at 16 & 18**

SafeGen enforces automatic expiry rules without storing dates of birth.

Mechanism:

1. Parent enters child’s month/year once during setup.
2. SafeGen hashes and salts that value.
3. A scheduled process compares the current date to **stored hashed age markers/expiry rules** and automatically changes status at 16 and removes entries at 18.
4. At the correct threshold, SafeGen:
   * Moves a 16-year-old from “restricted” to “limited access allowed”
   * Removes the entry entirely at 18

Properties:

* No DOB stored
* No personal data retained
* No identity linkage
* No manual maintenance required
* No risk of stale minor records

Result:\
**Every child is automatically delisted the moment law no longer applies — without holding or exposing any personal data.**
