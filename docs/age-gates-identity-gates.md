# Age gates should not become identity gates

EFF is right to warn that internet age gates are becoming a global civil-liberties threat. The default path is face scans, passports, government wallets, persistent identifiers, and identity checks for adults who are only trying to use the internet.

SafeGen takes the opposite view: child protection should not require everyone to prove who they are.

## The problem

Governments are pushing platforms toward age assurance. Australia, the UK, the EU, Brazil, Indonesia and Malaysia are already moving. If the default implementation becomes biometric or document-based age verification, the whole internet moves toward identity checkpoints.

That is bad policy and bad architecture.

## SafeGen's position

Protect known children. Do not identify every adult.

SafeGen is designed around parent-controlled child protection signals. The goal is to let services check whether a number is associated with a protected child without forcing ordinary users through face scans, passport uploads, or persistent identity systems.

## Civil-liberties test

A privacy-preserving age-assurance architecture should satisfy these rules:

- no biometric gate for ordinary internet access
- no passport or document upload just to participate
- no persistent identifier that follows adults across the web
- minimal data collection
- parent control where the issue is child account access
- regulator auditability without mass surveillance

## Why it matters

The policy fight is no longer child safety versus privacy. That framing is too crude.

The real fight is bad architecture versus better architecture.

Bad architecture asks everyone to prove who they are. Better architecture protects children without turning adults into identity subjects.

## Invitation

SafeGen should be assessed by civil-liberties groups, regulators, and platforms against this standard. If it fails, say so. If it passes, it offers a way to satisfy child-safety pressure without rebuilding the internet around identity gates.

Protect children. Do not identify everyone.
