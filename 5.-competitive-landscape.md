# 5. Competitive Landscape

Multiple age-assurance models exist.\
All have strengths and all fail in one or more critical areas:\
privacy, enforceability, scalability, interoperability, legal compliance, or bypass resistance.

SafeGen is the only architecture that delivers **enforcement without identity**, **accuracy without surveillance**, and **compliance without data-sharing**.

***

## **5.1 AI Age Estimation Approaches**

These systems analyse a user’s face using AI models to estimate age.\
Examples: face-based age estimation services, liveness detection tools, selfie-based ID matching models.

**Strengths**

* Fast and scalable
* No ID documents required
* Good at distinguishing children from adults under lab conditions

**Weaknesses**

* Requires face capture — politically and socially sensitive
* Creates biometric data-handling risk
* Vulnerable to lighting, gender, ethnicity variance
* Can be spoofed with high-quality photos or deepfakes
* Not legally acceptable for universal deployment in many jurisdictions
* Provides age estimation, not a _verifiable age attribute_
* Does not solve multi-platform enforcement (no cross-site signal)

**Bottom line**\
AI age estimation cannot deliver a **reusable, privacy-clean, cross-platform enforcement signal**.

***

## **5.2 ID-Upload Systems**

Users upload identity documents (passports, licences, Medicare cards) which are verified by third-party or platform KYC systems.

**Strengths**

* High accuracy
* Provides definitive age evidence

**Weaknesses**

* Extremely privacy-invasive
* High friction for users
* Risk of identity data breaches
* Creates centralised, high-value identity databases
* Often requires under-16s to handle ID documents illegally
* Impractical for routine or repeated checks
* Not scalable for children without ID

**Bottom line**\
ID-upload systems meet regulatory accuracy but **fail on privacy, safety, political acceptability, and scalability**.

***

## **5.3 Device-Side Biometrics**

Methods include:

* Face unlock
* OS-level face or fingerprint verification
* On-device age estimation by mobile hardware

**Strengths**

* Biometric data stays on the device
* Convenient for the user
* Strong liveness detection in some models

**Weaknesses**

* Platforms cannot rely on device biometrics (no cryptographic proof of age)
* Biometrics prove identity, not minority status
* Children routinely unlock parents’ phones
* No cross-platform age signal — every service must trust the device blindly
* No regulator audit trail
* No coverage for non-smartphone devices

**Bottom line**\
Device biometrics do not create a **trusted, transferable, auditable “minor = YES/NO” signal**.

***

## **5.4 Telco-Only Approaches**

Some proposals suggest telcos directly tell platforms whether a user is a minor.

**Strengths**

* Telcos have verified customer age (via mandatory KYC for SIMs)
* High accuracy

**Weaknesses**

* Telcos cannot legally share subscriber data
* Even de-identified sharing can violate metadata laws
* Telcos cannot expose phone numbers or customer attributes
* Creates liability for telcos
* Requires surveillance-style linkage between identity and platform activity
* Regulators will not allow telco → platform age attribute sharing directly

**Bottom line**\
A telco-only system is **legally impossible** without SafeGen’s privacy-preserving layer.
