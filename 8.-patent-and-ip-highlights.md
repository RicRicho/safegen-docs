# 8. Patent & IP Highlights

SafeGen’s architecture is protected through a combination of **core patents**, **process claims**, and **defensive publications**.\
The IP portfolio ensures SafeGen can be safely deployed nationally without fragmentation, imitation gaps, or incompatible implementations.

SafeGen patents do **not** involve identity collection, biometrics, or user surveillance.\
They protect **privacy-preserving enforcement infrastructure** — not personal data.

***

## **8.1 Clean Knowledge Event Patent**

The **Clean Knowledge Event (CKE)** is a patented method for generating and transmitting a **one-bit, non-identifying age attribute** that can be used by:

* social media platforms
* adult sites
* app stores
* telcos
* ISPs
* regulatory systems

**CKE patent coverage includes:**

* Creation of a one-bit age assertion (“minor = true/false”)
* Use of cryptographic signatures without identity linkage
* Separation of telco-held age attributes from platform activity
* Production of a privacy-preserving compliance signal
* Strong prevention of re-identification across institutions
* Auditable logs without PII

This patent forms the **foundation of SafeGen** — enabling enforcement without identity exposure.

***

## **8.2 Notification Layer Patent**

The notification layer converts a platform’s hashed query into a cryptographically verifiable enforcement signal.

**Coverage includes:**

* Privacy-preserving query/response structure
* Hash-based lookup systems that avoid identity reconstruction
* Multi-party separation ensuring no PII crosses boundaries
* Signed responses that are non-identifying yet regulator-auditable
* Structural prevention of cross-platform correlation
* Architecture that continues to limit re-identification even after compromise

This protects the core method that allows SafeGen to answer:

> “Is this user a minor?”\
> without knowing who the user is.

***

## **8.3 Hashing / Attribute-Proof Claims**

SafeGen’s data layer uses **irreversible hashing**, **telco-specific salting**, and **non-identifying attribute proofs**.

**Claims cover:**

* Methods for hashing phone numbers into non-reversible identifiers
* Use of salts to prevent cross-database correlation
* Hash-based age-attribute proofs without DOB, identity, or metadata
* Auto-delisting logic for 16/18 without storing personal dates
* Prevention of misuse through architecture-level data minimisation
* Systems that allow telcos to export age attributes _without exporting subscriber data_

This IP guarantees the **zero-identity model** that regulators require.

***

## **8.4 Defensive Publication Notes**

SafeGen employs a defensive publication strategy to:

* Prevent identity-based, biometric, or surveillance-heavy approaches from claiming similar ground
* Protect open, privacy-clean implementations
* Ensure competitors cannot patent harmful or privacy-invasive variants
* Clarify the boundary between SafeGen’s non-identifying architecture and unacceptable alternatives

Defensive publications document:

* Non-identifying signalling mechanisms
* Hash-based registries without personal data
* Architectures that prevent data-sharing between telcos and platforms
* Methods for regulator auditing without identity exposure

These publications ensure the ecosystem remains aligned with SafeGen’s **privacy-first principles** and cannot drift toward surveillance-based models.
